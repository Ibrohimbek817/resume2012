Grand Theft Auto V

Grand Theft Auto V (GTA 5) is an action-adventure game developed by Rockstar North and published by Rockstar Games. It was released in September 2013 for PlayStation 3 and Xbox 360, in November 2014 for PlayStation 4 and Xbox One, and in April 2015 for Microsoft Windows.

Gameplay

The game is played from either a third-person or first-person perspective, and its world is navigated on foot or by vehicle. Players control the three lead protagonists throughout single-player and switch between them both during and outside missions. The story is centered on the heist sequences, and many missions involve shooting and driving gameplay.

Red Dead Redemption II

Red Dead Redemption 2 (RDR2) is an action-adventure game developed and published by Rockstar Games. It was released in October 2018 for PlayStation 4 and Xbox One, and in November 2019 for Microsoft Windows and Stadia. The game is the third entry in the Red Dead series and is a prequel to the 2010 game Red Dead Redemption.

Gameplay

RDR2 is played from a first-person or third-person perspective, and the player controls Arthur Morgan, a member of the Van der Linde gang. The game features both single-player and online multiplayer components. The story is set in a fictionalized representation of the Western, Midwestern, and Southern United States in 1899, during the decline of the American frontier.

Grand Theft Auto IV

Grand Theft Auto IV (GTA IV) is an open-world action-adventure video game developed by Rockstar North and published by Rockstar Games. It was released on April 29, 2008, for PlayStation 3 and Xbox 360, and later for Microsoft Windows.

Gameplay

Grand Theft Auto IV is an open-world action-adventure game set in Liberty City, a fictional version of New York City. The game features a detailed urban environment, realistic mechanics, and a gripping storyline that follows Niko Bellic, an Eastern European war veteran who arrives in America seeking a fresh start but soon gets caught up in the criminal underworld. The gameplay in GTA IV revolves around an open-world exploration, mission-based progression, combat mechanics, vehicle driving, and various side activities. The open-world environment allows players to explore Liberty City freely. The city is divided into different boroughs, each inspired by real-life New York districts. Players can engage in various activities such as driving taxis, playing mini-games like bowling and darts, or even watching in-game television shows. The city feels alive with pedestrians, police, and dynamic weather changes. The wanted level system dictates police response; committing crimes increases the wanted level from 1 to 6 stars, with higher levels leading to stronger law enforcement pursuit. The game follows a mission-based structure, where Niko completes various tasks for different characters. Missions involve a mix of driving, gunfights, heists, and decision-making. Some missions present moral choices, where the player must decide the fate of certain characters, affecting the storyline. These choices add depth to the narrative and influence later interactions in the game.

Johnny Klebitz

Johnny Klebitz is a key character in the Grand Theft Auto IV universe and the protagonist of GTA IV: The Lost and Damned, one of the game's two expansion packs. He is the vice president (and later president) of The Lost MC, a notorious biker gang based in Liberty City.

Luis Lopez

Luis Lopez is the protagonist of GTA IV: The Ballad of Gay Tony, the second expansion pack for Grand Theft Auto IV. He is the bodyguard and business partner of Anthony "Gay Tony" Prince, the owner of some of Liberty City's most exclusive nightclubs. Unlike Niko Bellic or Johnny Klebitz, Luis is deeply involved in Liberty City's high-end nightlife and crime world, working for powerful crime bosses, drug dealers, and wealthy elites.

Niko Bellic

Niko Bellic is the protagonist of Grand Theft Auto IV and one of the most complex characters in the GTA series. He is an Eastern European war veteran who moves to Liberty City to escape his dark past and pursue the "American Dream." However, his journey quickly pulls him into the city’s violent criminal underworld.

Minecraft

Minecraft is a sandbox video game developed by Mojang Studios and first released in 2011. The game allows players to explore a blocky, procedurally generated world where they can mine resources, build structures, craft tools, and survive against hostile creatures.

Gameplay

Minecraft offers several game

Survival Mode – Players must gather resources, manage health, and fight off enemies like zombies and skeletons.

Creative Mode – Provides unlimited resources and allows players to build without restrictions.

Hardcore Mode – Similar to Survival Mode but with permadeath (one life only).

Adventure Mode – Designed for custom maps and exploration.

Spectator Mode – Lets players fly through the world without interacting with it.

Multiplayer & Community

Minecraft supports multiplayer gameplay, where players can join public servers, play with friends in LAN mode, or host their own worlds. The Minecraft community is one of the largest in gaming, with thousands of mods, skins, and custom maps available.

Minecraft Bedrock Edition

Minecraft: Bedrock Edition is a cross-platform version of Minecraft developed by Mojang Studios, written in C++, and designed for better performance and compatibility across multiple devices. It was first released in 2011 as Minecraft: Pocket Edition before evolving into Bedrock Edition.

Gameplay

Minecraft: Bedrock Edition offers an open-world sandbox experience where players can explore, build, and survive in a procedurally generated world made of blocks. The gameplay is similar to Java Edition but with some differences in mechanics and features.

Game Modes

1. Survival Mode

Players start with nothing and must gather resources, craft tools, and build shelters to survive.

Health and hunger bars require players to eat food and avoid damage from enemies.

Hostile mobs (zombies, skeletons, creepers, etc.) spawn at night or in dark areas.

Players can mine materials like stone, iron, and diamonds to craft better gear.

2. Creative Mode

Unlimited access to all blocks and items.

Players can fly and build anything without worrying about hunger or enemies.

Great for large building projects and experimenting with game mechanics.

3. Adventure Mode

Designed for custom maps with limited block-breaking abilities.

Used for challenge maps and story-based adventures.

4. Spectator Mode (Available in some versions)

Players can fly around and observe the world without interacting with it.

5. Hardcore Mode (Not available in Bedrock Edition)

Similar to Survival Mode, but with permanent death (only in Java Edition).

Java Edition

Minecraft: Java Edition is the original version of Minecraft, developed by Mojang Studios and released in 2011. It is written in Java and is primarily available for Windows, macOS, and Linux. This edition is known for its modding capabilities, advanced customization options, and complex Redstone mechanics.
